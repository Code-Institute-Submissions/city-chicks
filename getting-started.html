Getting Started

Congratulations on your decision to grow your own eggs at home! Below are 5 initial steps to get you started on your journey of keeping laying hens in your garden!

  • Setting up their home

    You will need to make sure you have enough space to house your chickens in your backyard Two square meters are required for every 4 hens you keep. Check out our Resourcessection for more information on where to buy a coop or how to build one yourself! Have water and food dispensers ready.

  • Acquiring the hens

    There are two popular options here, you could either buy brand new hens from a breeder, or if you want to be more adventurous, you can adopt hens after they have been rescued from battery farms. Rescue hens will only give eggs for a maximum of 1 year after their rescue, but they would be so pleased to live out their last days as free range hens!

  • Food and medicine

    Before you collect your hens, you will want to stock up on some feed, bedding, accessories and medicine. We have added some great links to the resources page for you to get acess to all the things you need.

  • Bringing the hens home

    Once you have your hen home set up, all you need to do is collect them and bring them home. Your hens will take a few days to settle in to their new environment, it is recommended that you keep an eye on them and visit them as often as you are able to, to make sure they are getting on alright.
