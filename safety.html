keeping your chicks safe

Growing your own hens comes with a few things to look out for, see below for tips on how to keep them safe and health so that you can have eggs forever!

Predators

Living in the city in Ireland are many urban foxes and cats. Foxes and cats will try to eat your chickens at the first available opportunity! Having a secure enclosure for your hens is one of the most important criteria for keeping your hens safe. You could build your own chicken coop using wood and wire, or you could purchase a readymade chicken run. Whichever you decide, check out our resources page for more info.

You may want to give them some time outdoors too. While you have your chickens roaming around the garden, you should employ the services of a trusty dog to keep an eye out for any chancers!

Pecking Order

Depending on the number of hens you decide to bring in to your garden, there will likely be a need for them to decide on their pecking order. As the name suggests, the pecking order is determined by pecking. The bigger, stronger hens will peck at the weaker ones to show them who's boss! This pecking can result in feathers being plucked out and sometimes even blood being drawn! Once the order has been decided, the hens should get on well, with each of the lower order hens knowing their place and keeping out of the way of their superiors.

Introducing new hens to your brood will mean that the pecking order will need to be re-established. Make sure to keep an eye out for the first few weeks and also be ready to separate the hens if the bullying gets out of hand.

Health and wellbeing

  • Food

    Your hens will love foraging around your garden or in their coop, but they will need supplementary chicken feed to ensure they get enough nutrituion to grow, stay strong and lay nutritious eggs. When your hens are ready to start laying (18 weeks and older) you should provide layer feed which is enriched with calcium to ensure strong egg shells (Check out our resources page for links to feed providers). You can also give them crushed up, clean egg shells in a separate container so that they have an additional source of calcium.

  • Clean Water

    Chickens should drink clean water to make sure they are kept healthy. It is recommended to clean out their water containers every day and replace with fresh water. Bacteria and other organisms can grow in stale water and this can make your chickens ill.

  • Apple Cider Vinegar

    Adding some apple cider vinegar to your chicken's drinking water helps to improve their gut health. The vinegar changes the ph of the water which helps to keep unwanted organisms at bay. Just add one tablespoons to every 2 litres of water.

  • Clean and dry bedding

    It's important to clear your coop of droppings and replace the straw bedding inside the laying hutch on a regular basis (two or three times a week, depending on the number of chickens you have). Parasites such as mites and poultry lice can thrive in a dirty coop and will make your chicken's lives very unpleasant.

  • Dust bath

    Chickens will dig shallow holes into the ground and rub their skin and feathers in the dirt. This is their way of keeping themselves clean and free of parasites. Make sure to have an area that allows them to have their daily dust baths, if you don't have access to the ground you can include a bucket of sand into the coop and they will use that.

  • Space for roaming

    Your chickens will want to roam free in your garden to try and find any insects that might be hiding about. They love having the freedom to move around and explore different areas. They will be much happier and will also lay healthier and more nutritious eggs.
